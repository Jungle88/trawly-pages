💼 Sales & Leverage
LEGAL SIGNAL
US 9th Circuit: companies can update their Terms of Service by email — and continued use means you agreed
The Court of Appeals just ruled that sending a TOS update by email constitutes valid notice, and continued use of a product implies acceptance of the new terms. It's a landmark precedent for SaaS contracts, subscription products, and anyone who's ever buried a change in an email they hoped customers wouldn't read too carefully.
Why it matters: This ruling dramatically simplifies contract management for SaaS founders. No more re-signing ceremonies or DocuSign flows every time terms change — a simple email + continued use = legally binding. It also means every enterprise customer you pitch needs to know their own TOS risk exposure.
🎯 Why this matters to you Trawly is about to onboard paying subscribers. This ruling means updating your Terms as the product evolves just requires an email + a changelog. More critically for Enviz: if you're dealing with enterprise contracts, understanding that your customers' TOS obligations can shift silently via email is a real due-diligence flag for any SaaS your team relies on.
