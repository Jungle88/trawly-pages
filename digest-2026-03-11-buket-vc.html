🔥 The Big One
FDA Removes Black Box Warning From HRT — 20 Years of Fear, Reversed
The FDA has officially removed the highest-severity drug warning from six menopausal hormone therapies. The black box warning, triggered by the flawed 2002 Women’s Health Initiative study, told a generation of women that HRT caused breast cancer, heart disease, and dementia. The FDA now says: for women under 60, or within 10 years of menopause onset, the benefits far outweigh the risks — including reduced all-cause mortality and fracture protection. This changes the HRT conversation in every GP’s office, globally.

Why it matters: The 2002 WHI study has been debunked repeatedly. The black box warning continued to chill prescribing and scare patients for 24 years. Its removal changes conversations in every GP’s office and gives doctors cover to do what the evidence has supported for years.
🎯 Why this matters to you You’re in early perimenopause awareness — exactly the window the FDA says has the strongest protective benefit from HRT. 40,000-Year-Old Cave Markings May Be Humanity’s First Writing System
Cave artefacts from Germany dated to 40,000 BCE — made by Aurignacian people — display structural properties strikingly similar to proto-cuneiform, the earliest known writing that emerged in Mesopotamia around 3300 BC. They aren’t a language, but they share the systematic, combinatorial logic of a writing precursor: consistent symbols, repeated patterns, evidence of deliberate encoding. University of Tübingen researchers say the structural parallels with later writing systems are impossible to ignore.
The dinner-table version: Writing might have been “invented” 40,000 years before the Mesopotamians — and we’ve been misattributing human symbolic intelligence to the wrong civilisation for centuries.

The HRT Story Isn’t About Hormones. It’s About 20 Years of Fear Built on a Flawed Study.
The 2002 WHI study scared a generation of women away from HRT. It was methodologically flawed — older participants, many years post-menopause, and risk-benefit figures applied to the wrong window. Twenty-four years later, the FDA has removed the warning. That’s not a regulatory footnote. That’s a correction of a public health mistake that affected tens of millions of women. The science was clear a decade ago. The bureaucracy just caught up — which means your GP now has cover to do what the evidence has supported for years.
