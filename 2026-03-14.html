🃏 Wildcard
Outside your usual read. Intellectually worth 2 minutes.
A Czech farmer used a 3,000-year-old Bronze Age weapon mold as a building block for 15 years
A man in Morkuvky, a small village in southeastern Czech Republic, found an unusual stone while renovating his barn in 2007. He used it as a building block. Curious about its strange markings, he eventually contacted local archaeologists. After analysis, archaeologist Milan Salaš of the Moravian Museum confirmed it was a Bronze Age casting matrix — a precision mold made from volcanic rhyolite tuff, designed to cast bronze spearheads 3,000 years ago. One side has a "precisely shaped negative impression" for casting a lance-shaped spearhead with a hollow base. It sat in a barn wall for over a decade before anyone noticed.
The dinner-table version: A farmer accidentally found a 3,000-year-old weapons factory instruction manual and used it as a doorstop.
🧒 Teach the kids: "3,000 years ago, people made bronze spear tips using this stone mould — like a Lego piece for weapons. Ask your 5-year-old: if you found a mysterious stone in our garden, what do you think it was for?"
