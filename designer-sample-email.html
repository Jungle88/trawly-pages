🧵 Thread of the Day
PG: "The Brand Age" — AI Makes Function Cheap. Brand Is the Moat.
Paul Graham published a new essay arguing we're entering "The Brand Age." His Swiss watch analogy: when quartz killed the need for Swiss precision in the 1970s, the watch industry pivoted to brand — and won. AI is doing the same thing to software now. Technical capability is commoditising. What's left? Who people trust. 391 HN points, 313 comments.
"The most expensive watches have always cost a lot, but why they cost a lot and what buyers got in return have changed completely."
— Paul Graham, paulgraham.com, March 2026
Read the full essay →
Key bullets:
• When output is abundant (AI era), trust becomes the scarce resource
• A brand is a reputation compressed into a signal that travels without you
• Content marketing is now table stakes — it no longer builds brand
• True brand = what people say about you when you're not in the room
• Implication: invest early in being known for one thing, clearly

👨‍👧 Dad Zone
Kitchen Table Maths — A Ruler and Bag of Rice Is All You Need
Saturday morning activity for the 5yo. No screens, no subscription. Grab a ruler and a bag of rice from the pantry and you have a complete hands-on measurement lesson. Works better than any app because the kid's hands are doing the learning, not their eyes.
This morning, if you have 20 minutes:
1️⃣ Get a ruler, bag of rice, and three different containers
2️⃣ Ask the 5yo: "which container holds the most? Guess first."
3️⃣ Fill with rice and count scoops. The guess vs reality = the lesson
4️⃣ The 2yo "helps pour" (good enough, he's learning too)
5️⃣ Measurement, estimation, prediction — all ticked
Source: @Dimitri83467364 thread on no-screen math for young kids. Simple, works, no prep time.
Also worth knowing: kids who grow up with touchscreens are developing their own typing habits before formal instruction. Early keyboard exposure may actually help — but physical hands-on play is still the foundation for spatial reasoning.
