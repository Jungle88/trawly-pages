Qatar Helium Shutdown Puts Chip Supply Chain on a Two-Week Clock
Qatar's helium production facility—responsible for 25% of global supply—just went offline for maintenance. The problem: helium is essential for semiconductor manufacturing, MRI machines, and rocket propulsion. Chip fabs typically hold 2-3 weeks of buffer stock. After that, production lines stop.
Why it matters: This isn't just semiconductor news—it's a reminder that critical supply chains depend on geographic chokepoints most people don't know exist. Two weeks of buffer is nothing when demand is this tight.
