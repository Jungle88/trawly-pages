🔥 The Big One
FDA Removes Black Box Warning from Hormone Replacement Therapy — After 23 Years
In February 2026, the FDA removed the black box warnings for cardiovascular disease, breast cancer, and dementia from all HRT products — ending 23 years of the most restrictive safety labelling. The decision applies to estrogen-alone therapy, combined estrogen-progestogen, systemic progestogen, and topical vaginal estrogen. The updated guidance reflects new evidence: women who start HRT within 10 years of menopause, particularly before age 60, show reduced all-cause mortality and fractures.
Why it matters: The 2002 WHI study created a generation of women (and their doctors) who were terrified of HRT. This regulatory reversal, 23 years later, acknowledges the original study's findings were misapplied — particularly to younger, perimenopausal women. It's a historic correction.
🎯 Why this matters to you You're in early perimenopause — which means you're in the exact window where HRT initiation shows the greatest benefit. This regulatory shift changes the risk-benefit conversation with your GP entirely. The "10-year window" guidance is now FDA-endorsed, not just menopause-specialist consensus.
🔖 Source: CancerNetwork, NPR, FDA press release · Published Feb 2026
🌿 Women's Health
NHS UPDATE
Non-Hormonal Menopause Drug Veoza Now Available on NHS
NICE has approved Veoza (fezolinetant) — a daily pill that blocks the nerve pathways in the brain that trigger hot flushes and night sweats. Around 500,000 UK women could benefit, particularly those who can't use HRT due to DVT, pulmonary embolism, or certain heart conditions. The drug is NOT recommended for women with active breast cancer or liver disease.
Why it matters: First non-hormonal option for vasomotor symptoms with robust clinical evidence. It works by blocking neurokinin-B signalling in the hypothalamus — a genuinely novel mechanism for hot flush treatment.

SUPPLY CHAIN
HRT Shortages Hit US as Demand Surges Post-FDA Decision
NPR reports rolling shortages of estradiol patches across US pharmacies. CVS confirmed manufacturers can't keep up with demand following the black box removal. Amneal Pharmaceuticals says they've seen "significant increase in demand" and are working to scale production. Transdermal estradiol (patches/gels) is the preferred delivery method now — bypasses liver metabolism, lower clot risk than oral.
🎯 Why this matters to you If you decide to start HRT, transdermal estradiol is the evidence-based first choice. Worth knowing the global supply situation — Australian shortages may follow US trends given shared manufacturers.

UVA Health and Flo study finds physical and emotional perimenopause symptoms are significantly more common in younger women than previously understood. CLINICAL
AI Predicts Chemo Response from Routine H&E Slides in Pancreatic Cancer
Precision oncology win: New AI analyses standard H&E pathology slides from pancreatic cancer biopsies to predict which chemotherapy works best — FOLFIRINOX vs gemcitabine combinations. The model showed significant survival edge in validation cohorts. The key insight: no extra tests needed, just smarter use of routine slides already being collected.
Why it matters: This is the "AI as augmentation" story done right — extracting more information from existing diagnostic workflows, not adding expensive new tests. If validated broadly, this changes pancreatic cancer treatment selection.

Largest Marine Reptile Ever Known — Discovered by an 11-Year-Old Girl
Ruby Reynolds was 11 when she found a fossil bone on a Somerset beach in 2020. That bone turned out to be from the largest marine reptile ever discovered — a 25-metre ichthyosaur from 202 million years ago. The species, now named Ichthyotitan severnensis, was longer than two school buses and likely weighed as much as a blue whale.
The dinner-table version: An 11-year-old girl found a bone on the beach that turned out to be from the biggest sea monster that ever lived. Scientists missed it for years. Kids are still better at finding things.

2025 Study: Montessori Kindergarten Shows Better Outcomes Across Multiple Domains
A 2025 study found that students randomly assigned to Montessori kindergarten registered better outcomes for reading, short-term memory, executive function, and social understanding at the end of their kindergarten year compared to traditional kindergarten.
