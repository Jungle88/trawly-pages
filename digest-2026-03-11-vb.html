🔥 The Big One
The FDA Just Reversed 20 Years of HRT Fear — Black Box Warning Officially Removed
Since 2002, a black box warning — the FDA’s most severe drug caution — had been attached to hormone replacement therapy, based on a flawed Women’s Health Initiative study that scared a generation of women and their doctors away from a genuinely beneficial treatment. Last month, the FDA made it official: the warning has been removed from six menopausal hormone therapies. The new position is unambiguous — for women under 60 or within 10 years of menopause onset, HRT benefits substantially outweigh risks, including reduced all-cause mortality, cardiovascular protection, and fracture reduction.
BioPharma Dive · CancerNetwork · Managed Healthcare Executive — February 12–13, 2026
Read on BioPharma Dive →
Why it matters: This is the largest regulatory shift in women’s health in two decades. The black box warning functionally ended HRT prescribing for a generation — not because the evidence warranted it, but because of risk-averse medicine. Its removal signals a sea change in how gynaecologists and GPs will now approach the menopause conversation.
🎯 Why this matters to you You’re in early perimenopause awareness, working full-time in cancer genetics and raising two boys under five — exactly the profile the FDA’s new guidance is designed to reach. You’re in the window where initiating HRT now, rather than waiting for symptoms to escalate, delivers the most durable cardiovascular and cognitive protection. The regulatory barrier that previously made doctors hesitant is now gone. This is the green light to have that conversation proactively — on your terms, not your symptom timeline.

A 519-case study in Cancers (MDPI) shows that adding Optical Genome Mapping alongside conventional karyotyping, FISH, and microarray identifies novel cytogenomic biomarkers, improves risk stratification, and reveals balanced structural rearrangements that current methods miss — particularly in haematological malignancies. International expert consensus recommendations on clinical OGM integration were published in early 2026 (PubMed 40304265). If your lab hasn’t been asked about OGM implementation yet, the conversation is coming.

Read the full study →
Why it matters: OGM extends conventional cytogenetics, it doesn’t replace it. 🎯 Why this matters to you As a hospital cytogeneticist, OGM is the most significant methodological addition to your toolbox in years.
