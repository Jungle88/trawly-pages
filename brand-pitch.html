1 Brand Discovery

What is Trawly?

Not a newsletter. A personal editor that trawls the internet for you — casting nets into the digital ocean and pulling up treasures worth your time.

The Insight

The metaphor of "trawling" is rich — it's about methodical, purposeful collection. Like a fisherman who knows the waters, Trawly navigates the vast internet and returns with only what matters.

The Problem

Every newsletter looks identical: light blue accents, generic sans-serif, disposable. How do you make a daily email feel like something worth opening — premium, not promotional?

The Opportunity

Trawly can own a visual space nobody occupies. An instantly recognisable signature element — like Stripe's gradient mesh or Notion's black+white+emoji — that makes every touchpoint unmistakably Trawly.

2 Direction A

"The Net"

Ownable. Editorial. Timeless.

The signature element is a geometric net pattern — derived from fishing trawl nets, abstracted into a sophisticated visual motif. Every brand touchpoint carries this DNA.

Signature Element — The Trawl Pattern

The "Why"

The trawl net is the literal tool of the trade — casting wide, pulling in treasures. Abstracted into a geometric diamond mesh, it becomes a sophisticated pattern language that's instantly ownable. Unlike generic gradients or illustrations, this pattern has semantic meaning tied directly to the brand name. It appears in backgrounds, card borders, dividers, and subtle textures — always recognisable, never overwhelming.

3 Direction B

"The Current"

Modern. Energetic. Tech-forward.

The signature element is flowing wave lines — representing the current of information, the constant flow of the internet that Trawly navigates and filters. Movement and momentum.

Signature Element — The Flow Lines

The "Why"

The internet is a constant current of information — overwhelming, relentless, endless. Trawly navigates these currents and extracts what's valuable. The flowing wave lines capture this sense of movement and momentum while feeling dynamic and modern. They work as subtle header decorations, section dividers, and animated accents — suggesting a product that's always working, always flowing, always current.
