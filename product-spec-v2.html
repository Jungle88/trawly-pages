The Manifesto

Trawly exists because the internet is drowning in noise and the best stuff is scattered across a dozen platforms. We trawl thousands of sources ‚Äî X, Reddit, Hacker News, blogs, YouTube, news sites ‚Äî so you don't have to. Every morning, you wake up to a beautifully crafted digest of what actually matters, written in a voice that feels like a smart friend catching you up over coffee.

We believe:

  • Your time is the most valuable thing you have. We save you hours every day.
  • Curation is an art. AI does the heavy lifting, but editorial taste drives everything.
  • Hyper-personalisation is the point. Not "top stories for tech people" ‚Äî stories that matter to you.
  • Trust is earned through honesty. When we get it wrong, we say so.

1. The Daily Digest

Flat Editorial Sequence

Each digest is a flat editorial sequence ‚Äî not rigid topic-then-widget sections. Content flows naturally, like a well-edited magazine. Category pills (small inline labels like AI/ML, Crypto, Parenting) identify each item's domain, but the sequence is editorially curated for reading flow.

Story Synthesis

This is core to what makes Trawly different from link dumps. When a topic is trending across sources, we synthesise ‚Äî pulling from X threads, Reddit discussions, news articles, and HN comments to create one narrative that's richer than any single source. Think AI-powered journalism, not aggregation.

Widgets

Widgets (weather, contrarian corner, rabbit hole, etc.) are included by default ‚Äî not user-selected. They're woven into the editorial sequence. If a subscriber consistently ignores a widget type, it gets phased out automatically based on behaviour data.

Personalisation

Throughout the digest, personalised callouts appear naturally: "This one's relevant because you mentioned you're building a SaaS..." Hyper-personalisation is the core product. "Your Kid Should Know" is an example of this, not a standalone feature.

Glossary Layer

Complex terms get inline parenthetical definitions on first use. No hover tooltips (email clients block JavaScript).

"The protocol uses ZK proofs (zero-knowledge proofs ‚Äî a cryptographic method that lets you prove something is true without revealing the underlying data) to verify transactions..."

2. Onboarding ‚Äî Radical Simplicity

One sentence. That's all we need to get started.

1

Email + One Sentence

"Tell us what you're into in one sentence."

"I'm a startup founder interested in AI, crypto, and I have two young boys."
2

AI Generates First Edition

Based on that single sentence, Opus generates a personalised first digest immediately. The subscriber sees what Trawly looks like for them before their first real email.

3

Optional "Tell Us More"

A gentle prompt: "Want to make it even better? Tell us more about yourself." Free text, not a form. Every detail improves the digest.

First-Week Personalisation Feedback

üí° During the first week, each digest includes a callout box: "We're still learning your taste. Anything feel off? Too much crypto? Not enough parenting stuff? Just reply or tap below to adjust." Easy opt-out per category (work / family / projects / industry).

3. Subject Lines

Warm, natural, like a text from a friend. Never formulaic. Never clickbait.

  • "That AI thing everyone's talking about? Here's what actually happened."
  • "Big day in crypto + something your boys would love"
  • "3 things worth knowing today (and one rabbit hole)"
  • "Quiet day on the internet. Here's the best of it."

Written by Opus with access to the subscriber's full taste profile.

4. @heytrawly Tagging Viral Loop

Users tag @heytrawly on X and Reddit to save content for their next digest.

  • @heytrawly ‚Üí Include in tomorrow's digest
  • @heytrawly more like this ‚Üí Include + amplify topic in taste profile
  • @heytrawly deep dive this tomorrow ‚Üí Include with expanded analysis

Growth: Gifting > Referring

For viral growth, we lean into gifting, not referral codes. "Send this digest to a friend" feels natural and generous. Gift a curated edition, not a discount code.

7. "We Were Wrong" Corrections

When Trawly gets something wrong, we proactively correct it in the next digest:

"Yesterday we said [X]. Turns out that's not quite right. Here's what actually happened: [correction]. We'd rather tell you we were wrong than let bad info sit."

Most newsletters never correct themselves. We do it prominently and without shame. Builds trust.

8. "Skip This" Honesty

On quiet news days (max 1/week, genuine only):

"Quiet day on the internet. Rather than pad this with filler, here's a shorter catch today."

9. Pause Sequence

Silent pause > guilt emails. We never send "we miss you" messages.

12. Brand Voice

Trawly sounds like a smart friend who reads everything.

  • Warm but not saccharine
  • Opinionated but fair ‚Äî we have takes, but we show our work
  • Conversational ‚Äî contractions, casual language, occasional humour
  • Honest ‚Äî we say when something's boring, when we're not sure, when we were wrong
  • Respectful of time ‚Äî we never waste a sentence
