🔥 The Big One
AI Just Cracked the Metastasis Code — Published in Cell Reports Yesterday
Researchers at the University of Geneva have developed MangroveGS, an AI tool that converts gene expression patterns into highly reliable predictions of whether a tumour will metastasize — across multiple cancer types. The team identified that cancer isn’t “anarchic” but follows structured biological rules, reactivating developmental programs switched off after early embryogenesis. MangroveGS translates those genetic signals into metastatic risk scores that outperform existing staging methods, potentially pointing toward new therapeutic targets.
📄 Cell Reports · University of Geneva · 21 Mar 2026
“Rather than being random, cancer appears to follow structured biological rules. The challenge is to find the keys to understanding its logic — and in the case of metastases, to identify the characteristics of the cells that will separate from the tumour to create another one elsewhere.” — Prof. Ariel Ruiz i Altaba, UNIGE
📊 Colon, breast, and lung cancers validated · cross-cancer-type prediction
Read on ScienceDaily →
Why it matters: Metastasis causes the vast majority of cancer deaths, yet no single genetic change has ever fully explained why some cells migrate and others stay put. MangroveGS is the first tool to reliably encode that risk from gene expression data — turning a diagnostic mystery into a predictive score clinicians can act on.
🎯 Why this matters to you As a hospital scientist in cancer genetics, MangroveGS is exactly the kind of tool that will land on your department’s radar in the next 12–18 months. The gene expression approach maps directly onto cytogenetic and genomic profiling workflows you already run — the clinical implementation question isn’t if, it’s how soon your lab starts validating against it.

HORMONAL HEALTH
HRT Cuts Type 2 Diabetes Risk by 21–35% in Perimenopause — But the Full Picture Is More Nuanced
A new evidence review published this week synthesises the latest data on HRT and metabolic risk. The diabetes finding is robust — the Endocrine Society estimates 11 fewer cases per 1,000 women per 5 years of HRT use in the 50–59 cohort. But the picture is messier for cardiovascular disease, blood pressure, and fatty liver: timing of initiation matters more than previously acknowledged, and oral estrogen vs transdermal has meaningfully different effects on cholesterol.
📖 DrSue.ca · 20 Mar 2026 · Evidence synthesis with JAMA, PubMed citations
“HRT does not result in weight loss … but may reduce the risk of type 2 diabetes by 21–35%. Bottom line: HRT may be a valuable treatment strategy for some women struggling with menopausal symptoms, but it should not be used solely for metabolic health reasons.”
Read the full review →
Why it matters: The diabetes risk reduction is one of the cleaner findings in perimenopause research — consistent across three independent datasets. But the nuance around timing (initiating HRT at perimenopause vs. post-menopause) is where most GPs get it wrong.
🎯 Why this matters to you You’re in early perimenopause and working full-time while raising two young boys — this research on metabolic risk timing is directly relevant to the HRT decision window you’re approaching. The “start early or don’t bother” finding on central fat deposition is the detail most women don’t hear until it’s too late to act on it.

ADAPTOGENS
Ashwagandha Is the One Adaptogen With Actual Mechanistic Evidence — Here’s What the Studies Actually Show
An evidence-led deep dive this week cuts through the adaptogen noise: most herbs in this category rest on traditional use alone, but ashwagandha has pharmacologically coherent mechanisms. The withanolides in ashwagandha appear to reduce HPA axis hyperactivation via GABA-A receptor effects, NF-κB inflammatory pathway inhibition, and direct modulation of cortisol synthesis in the adrenal cortex — meaning its effects on sleep, stress, and anxiety are testable, not just anecdotal.
Why it matters: For perimenopause specifically, HPA dysregulation is the mechanism linking chronic stress to hormonal disruption. Ashwagandha is one of the few herbal interventions where the proposed mechanism is specific enough to test against cortisol biomarkers — not just “I felt calmer.”

Sydney ML Engineer Used ChatGPT + AlphaFold to Shrink His Dog’s Terminal Tumour by 75%

Bull Sharks Don’t Just Hunt Alone — They Have Preferred Friends They Consistently Return To
After six years observing 184 bull sharks in Fiji, researchers discovered that these solitary predators actually form persistent social bonds. Certain individuals repeatedly sought out the same “friends” over time — a finding that challenges the entire lone-predator model of shark behaviour. The social network structure they found mirrors patterns seen in dolphins and elephants. Published this week, it’s upending how marine biologists think about apex predator cognition.
The dinner-table version: Bull sharks have best friends. They’ve been choosing the same companions for six years running. The ocean’s most feared predator has a social life.

CHILDREN’S SLEEP
Myofunctional Therapy Reduces Sleep Apnoea Severity in Children — And Most Parents Have Never Heard of It
Orofacial myofunctional therapy — targeted exercises for the mouth, tongue, and facial muscles — has been shown in multiple studies to reduce sleep apnoea severity in both children and adults. Unlike surgery (tonsillectomy/adenoidectomy), it works by retraining the neuromuscular patterns that cause airway collapse during sleep. In children who are habitual mouth-breathers, it addresses the upstream cause rather than the downstream anatomy. The Buteyko breathing method is an adjacent approach that also has clinical evidence for reducing nighttime mouth breathing in children.
📋 Evidence: News18 Medical / Healthcentral · Esther Greenberg, Myofunctional Therapist
“Mouth breathing can narrow the upper airway and is often shallow. Orofacial myofunctional therapy has been shown to reduce sleep apnoea severity in both children and adults.”
Read more on Healthcentral →
Why it matters: Myofunctional therapy is one of the very few non-surgical, non-invasive interventions for paediatric sleep apnoea with genuine clinical evidence behind it — and it addresses the cause (dysfunctional breathing patterns), not just the symptom.
🎯 Why this matters to you Your 5-year-old has mouth breathing and sleep apnoea at night, and you’re weighing ENT surgery against less invasive options. Myofunctional therapy is exactly what sits between “do nothing” and “operate” — it has RCT evidence, it’s available in Sydney, and it’s typically the intervention sleep specialists try before any surgical referral. Worth asking specifically about this before the next ENT appointment.
📝 Healthcentral · News18 Medical · multiple clinical sources
FOOD SAFETY
Tomatoes, Carrots, and Lettuce Are Absorbing Trace Medications From Wastewater-Irrigated Crops
Scientists studying crops irrigated with treated wastewater found that trace pharmaceuticals — including antibiotics and hormones — collect in the edible portions of plants, particularly in leaves. The concentrations are low, but the implications for children who eat more produce relative to body weight are worth understanding. Growing your own produce, especially leafy greens, from clean water sources sidesteps this entirely.
Why it matters: This study doesn’t suggest mainstream commercial produce is dangerous, but it does give evidence-based weight to something you probably already intuit: controlling the inputs to your food is a genuine health lever, not just a lifestyle preference.
